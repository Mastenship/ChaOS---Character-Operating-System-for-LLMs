=== SECTION: PERFORMANCE_STATE_INTEGRATION ===

[KEEP: Purpose & Role]
This COT defines how physiological arousal, psychological stress, biochemical conditions, and environmental context modulate skill execution in the moment. It applies Flow Theory, Yerkes-Dodson, motor control research, and cognitive load theory to produce:
- TEMPORARY_MODIFIERS overlays targeting [SKILLS][SKL_XX][EXECUTION]
- performance-related behavioral impulses
- rare, explicitly justified permanent SKILLS transitions only when SKILLS logic or MEMORY-defined events demand it

[KEEP: Relationship to Other Modules]
Informs: Skill execution quality via TEMPORARY_MODIFIERS, DECISION_SYNTHESIS impulses, MEMORY formation cues
Is Informed By: BODY biochemical markers, STRESS_CASCADE_RESOLUTION outputs, HEXACO trait modulation, ALTERATIONS temporary effects, TEMPORARY_MODIFIERS (existing overlays), narrative task difficulty

=== SECTION: AROUSAL_PERFORMANCE_MATRIX ===

[[LLM_INSTRUCTION]]: Convert arousal and task demands into modifier proposals.

Heuristic_Assessment:
- TASK_TYPE: [COGNITIVE_COMPLEX | FINE_MOTOR | GROSS_MOTOR | SOCIAL_PERFORMANCE | COMBAT]
- TASK_DIFFICULTY: [LOW | MODERATE | HIGH | EXTREME]
- AROUSAL_LEVEL_PROXY: derive from narrative, endocrine states, and existing modifiers:
  - [LOW | MODERATE | HIGH | OVERLOAD]

Typical_Effects:
- LOW arousal: under-engagement risk, slower reaction
- MODERATE arousal: best performance for many tasks
- HIGH arousal: boosts gross motor and simple actions; degrades complex cognition and fine motor
- OVERLOAD: choking, fragmentation, impulsive errors
